Use of Discord Account

We will ask you to link your Discord with Hack@UCF. This allows us to help expedite the sign-up process and, in the future, let you manage your membership from within Discord.

We also will use a list of server IDs to suggest ways to get involved in the club you may enjoy. We only check for a handful of popular Discords related to cybersecurity and UCF.

Privacy is very important to us. If you have any questions, email onboard@hackucf.org.

Onboard is a custom Python3 application that handles membership for Hack@UCF. It was written by Jeffrey to make it easier to get started with joining the club, since historically we had ~5 different steps that had to be filled out.

Onboard is powered by AWS Lambda, API Gateway, DynamoDB, Stripe, and the Discord API. When you link your Discord with Onboard, we collect some account metadata, such as your user ID, profile image, email, 2FA status, and username, as well as checking to see if you are in a small subset of servers, to help pre-fill options along the way.

Every person who links their Discord, regardless if they pay dues, will be given a Hack@UCF ID. This is a unique identifier that we can use in place of University identifiers (such as your UCF ID) to verify membership. You can access your Hack@UCF ID via the Onboard online portal. Note that if you swipe your UCF ID at an in-person event to verify membership, we may associate your UCF ID with your membership.

We also will, at the end of sign-up, ask for payment. We have a third-party vendor, Stripe, handle payments securely. We do not ever get your card information.
